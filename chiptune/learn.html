Chapter 1: The Sacred Interface

Understanding the Matrix

The tracker interface is like The Matrix, but with more bleeps and bloops. Each row represents a moment in time, and each column is a channel. Think of it as musical spreadsheet — BUT COOLER.

Row | CH1 CH2 CH3 CH4 ----+------------------------ 00 | C-4 01 50 --- --- --- 01 | --- --- C-3 02 60 --- 02 | E-4 01 40 --- --- --- 03 | --- --- --- G-4 03 70
Pro Tip: Each cell contains: NOTE + INSTRUMENT + VOLUME + EFFECT. It's like a tiny musical DNA sequence!

The Four Sacred Channels

  • CH1: Lead melodies (the main character of your song)
  • CH2: Bass lines (the foundation, the bedrock, the thicc beats)
  • CH3: Percussion (the spice, the rhythm, the bonk bonk)
  • CH4: Arpeggios/Effects (the sparkly bits that make everything ✨fancy✨)

Chapter 2: Keyboard Kombat

"It's over 9000!" - Your WPM after mastering these shortcuts

Basic Navigation (AKA "How to Move Around Like a Pro")

  • Arrow Keys - Navigate the pattern grid (your WASD equivalent)
  • Page Up/Down - Jump 16 rows (warp speed)
  • Home/End - Go to first/last row (teleportation)
  • Tab/Shift+Tab - Switch channels (dimension hopping)

Note Entry (The Ancient Ritual)

The keyboard is mapped like a piano, but sideways because trackers are rebellious:

Lower Row (Octave 4): Z=C S=C# X=D D=D# C=E V=F G=F# B=G H=G# N=A J=A# M=B Upper Row (Octave 5): Q=C 2=C# W=D 3=D# E=E R=F 5=F# T=G 6=G# Y=A 7=A# U=B
WARNING: Your fingers will hurt at first. This is normal. You are evolving into a higher life form.

Playback Controls

  • Space - Play/Pause (the most important key)
  • Enter - Stop (emergency brake)
  • F1-F4 - Select instruments (quick weapon swap)

Chapter 3: The Art of the Chiptune

What Makes a Chiptune Chip?

Chiptunes are the musical equivalent of pixel art - beautiful in their limitation. We're working with:

  • Square Waves: The bread and butter (sounds like old video games)
  • Pulse Waves: Square waves with attitude
  • Triangle Waves: Smooth operators (perfect for bass)
  • Noise: The chaos element (drums and percussion)
"Why limit yourself to 4 channels?" - Because constraints breed creativity, young padawan.

Your First Pattern

Let's create a basic 4/4 beat pattern. Follow along:

1. Add a kick drum on rows 00, 16, 32, 48 (Channel 3) 2. Add bass notes on rows 00, 08, 16, 24 (Channel 2) 3. Add melody on rows 00, 04, 08, 12 (Channel 1) 4. Sprinkle arpeggios everywhere (Channel 4)
Ancient Wisdom: Start simple. Even Mozart didn't compose symphonies on day one (and he didn't have trackers, the peasant).

Chapter 4: Advanced Wizardry

The ADSR Envelope (Not a Greeting Card)

ADSR controls how your sounds behave over time:

  • Attack: How fast the sound starts (0 = instant, higher = fade in)
  • Decay: How fast it drops from peak to sustain level
  • Sustain: The level it holds while the note plays
  • Release: How fast it fades when you let go
Quick ADSR Settings: - Percussive: A=0.01, D=0.1, S=0, R=0.1 (sharp hits) - Pad: A=0.3, D=0.2, S=0.7, R=1.0 (smooth and sustained) - Pluck: A=0.05, D=0.3, S=0.3, R=0.5 (guitar-like)

Pattern Sequencing

Once you have patterns, chain them together into songs. Think of patterns as LEGO blocks - you can reuse and rearrange them infinitely.
